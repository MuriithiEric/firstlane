General Questions

How much are the tuition fees in Poland?

International students can study at public and private universities in Poland at an average cost of 3,000 EUR per year for a bachelor’s degree, while fees for master’s degrees average at 4000 EUR per year.

Am I eligible to study in Poland, what do I need?

In Poland, undergraduate and graduate education can be obtained through universities without examination. However, your transcript should have some qualifications according to the department you want to study. For this, you can get information by sending an e-mail to our consultants: OUR EMAIL

Can I work while studying at university?

In Poland students can work while studying at university in Poland.

How should I choose my university?

After deciding on your department, you should look at which universities this department is in. In addition, you can choose the appropriate university by considering different evaluations such as the language proficiency required by the university, the level of the grades on your transcript. Since the process requires a special work for each student, you can ask our advisors: OUR EMAIL

How is the department decided?

You should compare the departments you want to study and your current qualifications and evaluate which university you have the chance to enter. For example, to study medicine in Poland, your grades such as physics, chemistry, biology must be at a sufficient level. For the aviation department, your different notes are taken into account. For this reason, you can get support from our consultants via OUR EMAIL with your transcript to decide on the department.

Technical Questions

Do I have to take an exam to study at university in Poland?

This may varies depending on the university and department you want to study. For example, some design and architecture universities require portfolios, while others only require language proficiency certificates.
