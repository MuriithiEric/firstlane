Introduction to Poland.

Poland is a large country with a rich history, a vibrant culture, and welcoming people. Poland is attracting more and more international students from Kenya and across Africa. The country offers world-class education at affordable tuition fees and extremely affordable cost of living for a European country along with a ready job market. Warsaw, Poland’s capital, and largest city is a modern, exciting hub with many universities offering a wide range of courses. Other major cities include Wroclaw, Krakow, Lodz, and Poznan.

Opportunities in Poland as an International Student

Studying in Poland is attractive not only academically, but also for the high quality of life available here. The country's economy has seen rapid growth over the past 20 years. It is a modern, clean, VERY SAFE and offers the opportunities available elsewhere. This is at a cost of living still much lower than in Western Europe, and international students are enrolling here in numbers.

Currency

The Polish currency is the Polish zloty (zł, PLN). One zloty is 100 groszy (gr). 1 Zloty is equal to 27 Kenya Shillings. The denominations are 10, 20, 50, 100 and 200 PLN banknotes in circulation as well as 1, 2 and 5 PLN coins and 1, 2, 5, 10, 20 and 50 groszy coins. Pro Tip:The groszy coins be quite a lot keep a coin section in your wallet would be helpful, but the easier option will be to use your bank card that will be delivered to you a week after we go open your bank account upon your arrival. Student accounts have zero charges for transactions and withdrawals.

Cost of living and Tuition Fees

International students can study at public and private universities in Poland at an average cost of 3,000 EUR per year for a bachelor’s degree, while fees for master’s degrees average at 6000 EUR per year. Poland’s cost of living is very low in comparison to most other European countries.

Note on Pricing

These are priced according to size of the room and features like, proximity to metro train, bus and tram stations, nearby grocery stores, presence of balcony etc. Single rooms are rooms occupied by one student in a shared apartment. Shared rooms are rooms occupied by two students in a shared apartment. Studio apartments are your own space with no shared amenities. In addition to the monthly rent the tenants pay for the utilities which include power, water, heating, and Wi-Fi bills. This is usually costs between 50 – 100 zloty monthly (1,350 – 2,700 KSH).

Student Discounts

Most cinemas, theatres, and museums as well as hairdressers, some restaurants, and cafes offer special prices for students. To receive a discount, please show your student ID to staff upon making payment. In Poland, students receive 50% discount on public transportation, including long-distance trains.
Pro Tip: Please make sure you have your valid student ID if you wish to benefit from a student discount otherwise you may receive a fine.
